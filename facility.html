Private Room

A private room in a hospital is a room that is typically used for patients who are able to pay for more privacy, or for patients who need to be isolated from the rest of the hospital for medical reasons. Private rooms usually have more amenities than regular hospital rooms, and they may be located in a different area of the hospital.

X-ray Rooms

Patients may be referred to the X-ray room by their physicians for a variety of diagnostic purposes, such as identifying broken bones, detecting tumors, or identifying conditions affecting the lungs, heart, or other organs. Trained radiologic technologists typically operate the equipment and help position the patient appropriately to obtain the necessary images. After the images are acquired, radiologists or other medical professionals review and interpret the images to provide a diagnosis or treatment recommendation.
